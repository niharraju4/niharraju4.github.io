Spacy

Definition: SpaCy is an advanced open-source library for Natural Language Processing (NLP). What it does: Provides tools for tokenization, named entity recognition, and dependency parsing. Application: Supports multiple languages, making it versatile for text processing tasks. Advanced Features: Seamlessly integrates with deep learning frameworks like TensorFlow and PyTorch.

StopWords

Definition: Stopwords are commonly used words like 'the,' 'is,' and 'and.' What they do: They are often removed during text preprocessing to reduce noise. Efficiency: Helps in reducing data size for faster processing. Special Use Cases: Retaining stopwords may be necessary for tasks requiring contextual understanding.

Bag of Words

Definition: A model that represents text as word frequency counts. What it does: Converts text into numerical format, ignoring grammar and word order. Application: Used in text classification and clustering tasks. Limitation: Fails to capture semantic relationships between words.

stemming

Definition: A process to reduce words to their root form by removing suffixes. What it does: Treats similar words like 'running' and 'runs' as the same. Efficiency: Simplifies text analysis by normalizing variations. Caution: Can lead to over-reduction, impacting the accuracy of results.

Definition: Assigns grammatical categories (noun, verb, adjective) to words. What it does: Helps understand sentence structure and meaning. Application: Essential for tasks like syntactic parsing and sentiment analysis. Integration: Often combined with dependency parsing for deeper insights.

Definition: Identifies entities like names, dates, and locations in text. What it does: Extracts structured information from unstructured text. TF-IDF

Definition: A statistical technique to measure word importance in a document. What it does: Emphasizes rare terms while downplaying frequent ones. Application: Widely used in keyword extraction and document ranking. Relevance: Crucial for search engine optimization and text similarity tasks.

Word2Vec

Definition: A neural network-based model that converts words into vectors. What it does: Captures semantic relationships between words in vector space. Application: Used in recommendation systems and sentiment analysis. Advanced Features: Produces embeddings with properties like semantic clustering.
